## Jul 3 Options

**Goal:** One paid outdoors adventure + a late soak, without crisscrossing the state.

**Reality check:** There is no via ferrata in Gunnison / Crested Butte. Local paid whitewater is on the **Taylor** and **Gunnison** rivers (Almont / Taylor Canyon), not the Arkansas. Anything “developed” for hot springs is a **drive** — the win is keeping activity + soak in the **same corridor**.

**Drive hub:** Airbnb at 461 County Rd 11 → **~25 min** to Almont (rafting) · **~2 hr** to Ouray / Ridgway (ferrata + west-side springs) · **~45 min** to Crested Butte (primitive springs)

---

### Recommended combos *(ranked)*

#### 1. Ouray day — best via ferrata + soak logistics *(top pick if you want ferrata)*


| Block   | What                                                                                | Drive from prior stop |
| ------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------- |
| Morning | Drive to Ouray                                                                      | ~2 hr from Airbnb     |
| Midday  | **Ouray Via Ferrata** (guided) — Downstream = beginner-friendly; Upstream = spicier | In town               |
| Late PM | **Ouray Hot Springs Pool** — canyon soak, open until 10 PM in summer                | In town (~0 min)      |
| Night   | Drive home                                                                          | ~2 hr to Airbnb       |


- **Outfitter:** [Cirque Guides](https://cirqueguides.com/climb/via-ferrata/) — Downstream ~4 hr; departures 8:00 / 10:00 / 12:30 AM · ~$159–249/person (group of 4–5)
- **Soak:** [Ouray Hot Springs Pool](https://www.visitouray.com/ourayhotspringspool) — ~$30/adult · re-entry same day with receipt
- **Why it works:** ~4 hr total driving; activity and soak share one town. No Buena Vista detour.
- **Sample day:** Leave ~7:30 AM → 10:00 AM ferrata → ~2:30 PM late lunch in Ouray → pool ~6:00–9:00 PM → home by ~11:30 PM
- **Long day warning:** ~8–9 hrs on the road + activity time. Start early.

#### 2. Taylor rafting + west-side soak — best “stay local” adventure, developed evening soak


| Block     | What                                                            | Drive from prior stop              |
| --------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Morning   | **Upper Taylor Extender** (Class III)                           | ~30 min to Taylor Canyon boathouse |
| Afternoon | Drive west on US-50                                             | ~2 hr to Ridgway / Ouray           |
| Evening   | **Orvis Hot Springs** (Ridgway) *or* **Ouray Hot Springs Pool** | Orvis ~10 min north of Ouray       |
| Night     | Drive home via same US-50 corridor                              | ~2 hr to Airbnb                    |


- **Outfitter:** [Scenic River Tours](https://scenicrivertours.com/trips/upper-taylor-extender/) — ~3 hr on water · ~$108/person · min age 10 / 90 lb · boathouse ~30 min from Gunnison / Crested Butte
- **Alt outfitter:** [Three Rivers Resort](https://3riversresort.com/rafting/) (Almont)
- **Soak:** [Orvis Hot Springs](https://orvishotsprings.com/) — day soak 9 AM–9 PM, last entry 8 PM · clothing-optional outdoor pools · ~$20–30/person range (confirm current rate)
- **Why it works:** Morning is truly local; afternoon/evening drive is **one direction** (west) for both soak options.
- **Tradeoff:** You still do ~4 hr driving, but you avoid “raft east, soak southeast (BV)” backtracking.
- **Sample day:** 8:30 AM Taylor trip → ~12:30 PM done → lunch in Gunnison or Almont → leave ~2:00 PM → soak ~5:00–8:30 PM → home ~10:30–11:00 PM

#### 3. Taylor rafting + primitive soak — least driving, weakest “hot spring” vibe


| Block     | What                                                                      | Drive from prior stop             |
| --------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Morning   | Upper Taylor Extender                                                     | ~30 min                           |
| Afternoon | **Ranger Hot Spring** or **Cement Creek Hot Spring** (near Crested Butte) | ~45 min from Airbnb / Taylor area |


- **Why it works:** Activity and soak are both in the **Gunnison Valley** (~1–1.5 hr total driving for the day).
- **Tradeoff:** Natural springs run **~77–81°F** — pleasant river dip, not a hot resort. Pack towels; check access / crowding; no facilities.
- **Best for:** Group that wants maximum river time and minimum windshield time, and is fine with lukewarm / rustic.

#### 4. Telluride via ferrata + Orvis — epic, more moving parts


| Block   | What                                        | Drive from prior stop    |
| ------- | ------------------------------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Morning | Drive to Telluride                          | ~2.5 hr from Gunnison    |
| Midday  | **Telluride Via Ferrata** (guided, ~4–5 hr) | —                        |
| Evening | **Orvis Hot Springs**, Ridgway              | ~45 min from Telluride   |
| Night   | Home via US-50                              | ~2 hr Ridgway → Gunnison |


- **Outfitter:** [Telluride Mountain Guides](https://www.telluridemountainguiding.com/via-ferrata) — ~$185/person (party of 4)
- **Why consider:** Stunning ferrata above Telluride; Orvis is a legit evening soak nearby.
- **Why it’s #4:** Longest day (~9+ hr driving); July afternoon thunderstorms are common on the ferrata — book **morning** start only.
